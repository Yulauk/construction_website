{{ _('January') }} 24, 2024

A major apartment renovation includes various stages, starting from preparatory work and ending with finishing touches. Here is a detailed description of the rough construction works:

1. Demolition Works:
  • Removal of old wallpapers, floor coverings, and ceiling finishes.
  • Demolition of old plumbing and wiring.
  • Possible removal of partitions or layout changes.
2. Priming Works:
  • Surface preparation for subsequent finishing materials.
  • Application of primer on walls and ceilings.
3. Construction Works:
  • Construction of new partitions or removal of old ones.
  • Possible room layout changes.
  • Replacement or reinforcement of floors.
4. Electrical Works:
  • Installation of new wiring.
  • Installation of sockets, switches, and light fixtures.
  • Preparation for connecting household appliances.
5. Plumbing Works:
  • Replacement or updating of pipes and fittings.
  • Installation of new plumbing fixtures (bathtub, toilet, sinks).
  • Installation of plumbing devices.
6. Heating and Ventilation:
  • Installation of radiators and heating pipes.
  • Installation of a ventilation system or reconstruction of an existing one.
7. Plastering and Painting Works:
  • Alignment of walls and ceilings.
  • Puttying and sanding of surfaces.
  • Painting or applying decorative coatings.
8. Flooring Works:
  • Installation of new floor coverings (laminate, parquet, tiles).
  • Floor insulation if necessary.
9. Doors and Windows:
  • Installation of new doors and windows if necessary.
  • Treatment, painting, or cladding.
10. General Works:
  • Installation of baseboards, casings, and other finishing elements.
  • Preparation for subsequent finishing (if not included in rough construction works).
11. Cleanup:
  • Cleaning the premises from construction debris.
  • Preparation for the final finishing.
These stages represent the main tasks in a major apartment renovation. Each stage requires careful planning and coordination to achieve a successful outcome.
